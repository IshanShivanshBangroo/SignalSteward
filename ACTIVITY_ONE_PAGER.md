# Signal, Edit, Support — simple class explanation

**Developed by Ishan • Designed by Nicole**

This is a short live class activity built around three ideas from the readings.

Students enter their names and chat with a digital friend for three short turns.

The first turn asks what kind of public update is actually useful when something is unfolding quickly. This is the signal lens.

The second turn asks how a shared Wikipedia-style page should be coordinated so that more contributors improve quality instead of creating noise. This is the edit lens.

The third turn asks which social ties matter first and what should stay public or bounded. This is the support lens.

After the chat, each student gets a stance number, a one-line summary, and a short public excerpt that appears on the live wall with their name.

Then the facilitator clicks **Build balanced teams**. The system always splits the room into two debate groups by count, so 14 students become 7 and 7.

The debate prompt is:

> In a fast-moving community event, should the system prioritize open signaling and broad participation, or tighter coordination and selective visibility?

Each team gets a short prep period and sends one spokesperson to debate.
